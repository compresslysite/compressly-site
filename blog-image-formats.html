Best Image Format for Websites in 2025: JPEG vs PNG vs WebP

By Compressly Team · 6 min read

Choosing the wrong image format can slow down your website, hurt your SEO, and frustrate your visitors. This guide breaks down the three main formats — JPEG, PNG, and WebP — so you can make the right choice every time.

The 3 Main Image Formats

JPEG
Photos & Complex Images

Best for photographs and images with many colors. Small file size with good quality.

✅ Small file size

✅ Universal support

❌ No transparency

❌ Lossy compression

PNG
Logos & Graphics

Best for logos, icons, and images that need transparent backgrounds.

✅ Transparency support

✅ Lossless quality

❌ Larger file size

❌ Slow loading

WebP
⭐ Best Overall

Google's modern format. Best quality and smallest file size. Supports transparency.

✅ 30% smaller than JPEG

✅ Supports transparency

✅ Best for SEO

❌ Some old browsers

Which Format Should You Use?

  • Photos & Blog Images → Use JPEG at 80% quality
  • Logos & Icons → Use PNG or SVG
  • All Website Images → Use WebP for best performance
  • Social Media → Use JPEG

How Image Format Affects SEO

Google ranks faster websites higher. Large images slow down your site and hurt your SEO score. Using the right format and compressing your images can improve your Google PageSpeed score significantly.
